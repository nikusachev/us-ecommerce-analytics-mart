# Metric Definitions

## General Business Rules

The project uses an order and item-level e-commerce dataset with the following analytical layers:

* `raw` — source CSV data loaded into PostgreSQL.
* `stg` — cleaned staging views with prepared fields.
* `mart` — analytical fact views for metrics and Power BI.

Sales and revenue metrics are calculated only for delivered orders:

```sql
is_delivered = 1
```

Canceled orders are excluded from sales metrics and used separately for cancellation analysis.

---

## Order Metrics

### Total Orders

Total number of orders in the dataset.

```sql
COUNT(*)
```

Source:

```text
mart.fct_orders
```

Grain:

```text
1 row = 1 order_id
```

---

### Delivered Orders

Number of successfully delivered orders.

```sql
COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE is_delivered = 1)
```

---

### Canceled Orders

Number of canceled orders.

```sql
COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE is_canceled = 1)
```

---

### Cancellation Rate

Share of canceled orders among all orders.

```sql
canceled_orders / total_orders * 100
```

---

## Revenue Metrics

### Gross Item Revenue

Revenue before discounts.

```sql
SUM(gross_item_revenue)
```

At item level:

```sql
price
```

At order level:

```sql
SUM(price)
```

---

### Discount Amount

Amount of discount applied to an item.

```sql
price * discount_rate
```

---

### Net Item Revenue

Revenue after discount.

```sql
price - discount_amount
```

or:

```sql
price * (1 - discount_rate)
```

---

### Net Revenue

Total revenue after discounts, calculated only for delivered orders.

```sql
SUM(net_item_revenue) FILTER (WHERE is_delivered = 1)
```

---

### AOV — Average Order Value

Average revenue per delivered order.

```sql
net_revenue / delivered_orders
```

Source:

```text
mart.fct_orders
```

---

## Profitability Metrics

### Cost

Product cost from the product dimension.

```text
stg.stg_products.cost
```

---

### Gross Profit

Revenue after discount minus product cost.

```sql
net_item_revenue - cost
```

Source:

```text
mart.fct_order_items
```

Business assumption:

```text
1 row in order_items = 1 sold item unit
```

---

### Gross Margin %

Share of gross profit in net revenue.

```sql
gross_profit / net_revenue * 100
```

---

## Customer Metrics

### Customer Identifier

For customer analytics, repeat purchases, retention and customer value, the project uses:

```text
customer_unique_id
```

`customer_id` is used for joining orders to customer records, while `customer_unique_id` represents the real customer across multiple orders.

---

### Customer Value

Total delivered-order revenue generated by a customer.

```sql
SUM(net_item_revenue)
```

Grain:

```text
1 row = 1 customer_unique_id
```

---

### Orders Count per Customer

Number of delivered orders made by a customer.

```sql
COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)
```

---

### Repeat Customer

A customer with more than one delivered order.

```sql
CASE
    WHEN COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) > 1 THEN 1
    ELSE 0
END
```

---

### Customer Lifetime Days

Number of days between the first and last delivered purchase.

```sql
MAX(purchase_date) - MIN(purchase_date)
```

---

## Retention Metrics

### Cohort Month

The month of the customer's first delivered purchase.

```sql
MIN(order_month)
```

Grain:

```text
1 row = 1 customer_unique_id
```

---

### Active Customer

A customer with at least one delivered order in a given month.

For retention, the intermediate grain is:

```text
1 row = 1 customer_unique_id + 1 active month
```

Multiple orders in the same month are counted as one customer activity.

---

### Month Number

Number of months between the cohort month and the active order month.

```sql
(year difference * 12) + month difference
```

Example:

```text
cohort_month = 2024-01-01
order_month  = 2024-03-01
month_number = 2
```

---

### Cohort Size

Number of customers in the cohort at month 0.

```sql
active_customers where month_number = 0
```

---

### Retention Rate %

Share of cohort customers who returned in a given month number.

```sql
active_customers / cohort_size * 100
```

At `month_number = 0`, retention should always be:

```text
100%
```

---

## Category Metrics

### Items Sold

Number of sold item rows.

```sql
COUNT(*)
```

Source:

```text
mart.fct_order_items
```

---

### Orders Count by Category

Number of unique orders containing a category.

```sql
COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)
```

---

### Category Revenue Share %

Share of a category in total monthly revenue.

```sql
category_net_revenue / monthly_total_revenue * 100
```

Used to analyze category contribution and seasonal shifts.

---

## Important Grain Rules

### `mart.fct_orders`

```text
1 row = 1 order_id
```

Used for:

* total orders
* delivered orders
* canceled orders
* cancellation rate
* AOV
* customer value
* repeat purchase
* retention

---

### `mart.fct_order_items`

```text
1 row = 1 order item
```

Used for:

* product analytics
* category analytics
* seller analytics
* gross profit
* gross margin
* item-level revenue

---

## Unsafe Direct Joins

The following joins may multiply rows if used without aggregation:

```text
orders + raw order_items + raw order_payments
customers + raw geolocation
```

Before joining to order-level marts:

* `order_items` must be aggregated to `order_id`.
* `order_payments` must be aggregated to `order_id`.
* `geolocation` must be aggregated to `zip_code_prefix`.
